TEE & AI-Enabled Applications
IC3 logo
Supported by IC3
Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) create hardware-isolated zones where code runs privately — even the machine's owner can't see inside.
This track explores what becomes possible when you combine TEEs with AI agents, data pipelines, and coordination protocols: from delegating credentials without exposing passwords, to enforcing NDAs with AI agents, to building privacy-preserving data marketplaces.

Dstack is the shared infrastructure layer across this track — a decentralized TEE cloud that several of these papers build on top of.

Cryptographic Primitives & Identity
IC3 logo
Supported by IC3
The building blocks of trust without centralization.
This track covers threshold cryptography (splitting secrets across multiple parties), privacy-preserving identity and login systems, ZK proving acceleration, and the practical realities of TEE attestation. These are the primitives that other applications — including those in Tracks 1 and 3 — are built on.

Bonus Track: The Cohort that Builds Itself
IC3 logo
Supported by IC3
Build a tool that your peers can't live without.
Researchers and builders collaborate every day — sharing data, delegating access, tracking contributions, protecting IP — using infrastructure that wasn't designed for privacy or trust. This track challenges you to fix that.

Your users are people like you. Build something that makes collaborative research and development meaningfully better, using the cryptographic and TEE primitives from the reading list.

For example: imagine a TEE-private sandbox where teams submit meeting transcripts or proprietary data for processing — without exposing raw content to other teams or organizers.

The reading list is full of starting points (examples below), but we're not prescribing solutions. If it solves a real collaboration pain point and leverages the research, we want to see it.

Cycles Protocol — process attribution data as a graph, clearing contributions and dependencies across teams
Props — TEE-based meeting transcript aggregator for cross-team work, private data processing by the host
Conditional Recall — share cloud credentials, API keys, or dev resources across a team without exposing secrets
Thetacrypt — threshold-signed team wallets, shared custody of project assets
Anonymous Self-Credentials — Sybil-resistant hackathon registration without doxxing participants
NDAI Agreements — teams pitching ideas to judges/sponsors without risking IP expropriation
Persistent BitTorrent Trackers — distribute datasets and shared codebases with verifiable contribution tracking
Your project doesn't need to be fully deployed by the end of the hackathon — but it should be clear who would use it, and why.
